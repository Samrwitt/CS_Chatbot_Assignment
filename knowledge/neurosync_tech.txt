An n-gram is a contiguous sequence of n items from a given sample of text or speech. The items can be phonemes, syllables, letters, words or base pairs according to the application. The n-grams typically are collected from a text or speech corpus. When the items are words, n-grams may also be called shingles. 
A unigram is an n-gram of size 1; a bigram is of size 2; a trigram is of size 3. 
In the NeuroSync project, we use an AdvancedPredictor that implements linear interpolation smoothing. This technique combines unigram, bigram, and trigram models to calculate the probability of the next word. Linear interpolation is effective because it handles data sparsity—if a trigram has never been seen, the model falls back to bigrams and unigrams to make a reasonable prediction.
The "Neuroplasticity" feature in NeuroSync simulates the brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections throughout life. In our code, this is represented by the `learn_from_user` method, which increases the weight of words and patterns used by the user, making the bot more personalized over time.
